Below The Rage

I see you hear that distant roar?
I know you hear the ocean’s rage.
But, continue past the mad rampage,
Beyond and down below, find the ocean’s floor.

Back above, the waves whip and churn.
Yet below, they hold you and embrace.
Gently, silently, bringing in new space.
Does your heart begin to yearn?

There is life upon this seabed.
Flurries of yellow and gray sway through,
And cities of coral spread vast in the deep blue.
Do you start to prefer these depths instead?

But, when you surface and return to home,
The sky is pouring and the sun is gone.
It feels hostile, just as if there were no dawn.
This wasteland will be a mad life to roam.

So retreat! Dive underneath the world’s edge!
Recall the tranquil depths as you descend!
The peaceful fishes will be your new friend.
And you’ll never return, this you’ll pledge.

So when you hear the distant roar,
When you feel the ocean’s rage.
Find a way to move past that page,
And look towards the ocean’s door.
